# HR-Analytics-

An interactive Employee Retention Dashboard built with Power BI, analyzing attrition rates, income bands, promotion gaps, work-life balance, and departmental trends across 50K employees.

An interactive HR Analytics Dashboard built with Power BI, providing deep insights into employee attrition, income distribution, promotion gaps, work-life balance, and departmental performance across a workforce of 50,000 employees.

📌 Overview

Employee retention is one of the most critical challenges for any organization. This dashboard provides HR teams and business leaders with a comprehensive, data-driven view of attrition patterns and workforce health metrics — enabling smarter decisions around compensation, promotion policies, and employee well-being.

📊 Dashboard Sections
1. 💰 Monthly Income & Attrition by Income Band

Compares Sum of Monthly Income vs Average Attrition Flag across income bands: High, Upper-Mid, Mid, Lower-Mid, and Low.
Key Insight: The High income band has the greatest payroll concentration (~0.50bn), while attrition remains relatively uniform (~0.50) across all bands — suggesting income alone does not strongly predict attrition.

2. 🕐 Promotion Gap & Attrition Rate

Analyzes Employee Count and Attrition Rate (%) by Promotion Gap Category: Recently Promoted, Moderate Gap, Long Gap, and Very Long Gap.
Key Insight: Employees recently promoted form the largest group (~32K), while attrition rates remain consistent (~50%) across all promotion gap categories.

3. ⚖️ Work-Life Balance by Job Role

A stacked bar chart showing Employee ID Count broken down by Work-Life Balance Category (Poor, Good, Fair, Excellent) across all Job Roles.
Job roles covered: Sales Executive, Healthcare Representative, Manager, Research Director, Research Scientist, Sales Representative, Developer, Manufacturing Director, Human Resources, Laboratory Technician.
Key Insight: Most roles show a mixed distribution of work-life balance ratings, highlighting role-specific wellness opportunities.

4. 🏢 Average Working Years by Department

A donut chart comparing Average Total Working Years across departments: Software, Sales, Support, Hardware, and Human Resources.
Key Insight: Average working years are nearly equal across all departments (~20.45–20.65 years), indicating a stable, experienced workforce.

5. 📉 Attrition Flag by Department

A horizontal bar chart showing Average Attrition Flag by department.
Key Insight: All departments show attrition flags between 0.49 and 0.51, suggesting attrition is a company-wide issue rather than department-specific.

6. 🔍 Selected Filter: Male Research Scientist

A spotlight card showing a filtered view for Male Research Scientists with an Average Hourly Rate of $115.16.
